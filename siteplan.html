Project Site Plan – Week 5

Project: Water for All Peru

Author: Joaquín Cáceres Bello

Site Name

Water for All

This name represents the goal of the website: to raise awareness and promote initiatives that help bring clean water to all communities in Peru. It is simple, meaningful, and easy to remember.

Site Purpose

The purpose of this website is to inform visitors about the current water situation in Peru, especially in rural areas, and to encourage participation in local and national efforts to improve access to clean water. The site will include educational resources about water conservation, an interactive map showing areas with limited access, and a submission form where users can share information about community projects or water-related problems.

Scenarios

  1. A visitor wants to learn which regions in Peru have the most limited access to clean water.
  2. A student or teacher is looking for educational material about water conservation for classroom use.
  3. A community leader wants to share their local initiative that helps provide clean water to families.

Color Schema

Color Hex Code Usage
Deep Blue #0077B6 Header background and main buttons, representing clean water.
Light Blue #00B4D8 Section highlights and links, giving a fresh, calm feel.
Soft Aqua #CAF0F8 Page background to maintain light contrast and readability.
Dark Navy #03045E Text headers for strong contrast and legibility.

Typography

Primary Font: Poppins

Used for headings and navigation links for a clean, modern look.

Secondary Font: Open Sans

Used for paragraph text to ensure good readability.

Wireframe Description

Home Page Layout

Mobile View:

  • Header with logo and navigation menu (hamburger icon).
  • Hero image with title and short description.
  • Sections: "About the Problem", "Interactive Map", "Community Stories", and "Contact Form".
  • Footer with social links and copyright.

Desktop View:

  • Full navigation bar at the top.
  • Hero image across the page.
  • Two-column layout for "About" and "Map" sections.
  • Community stories displayed in a responsive grid.
  • Contact form at the bottom before the footer.

Target Audience

  • Peruvian citizens interested in water accessibility issues
  • Students and teachers seeking educational resources
  • Community leaders and NGO workers
  • Government officials and policy makers
  • International organizations focused on clean water initiatives

Key Features

  • Interactive Map: Visual representation of water accessibility across Peru
  • Educational Resources: Materials about water conservation and best practices
  • Community Stories: Real testimonials and success stories from local initiatives
  • Submission Form: Platform for users to report water issues or share projects
  • Responsive Design: Optimized for mobile, tablet, and desktop devices
