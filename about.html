Understanding Peru's Water Crisis

The facts, the challenges, and the path forward

The Current Situation

30 Million People

Peru's population, with approximately 8 million lacking adequate access to clean water.

70% Rural Impact

Rural communities are disproportionately affected, with only 51% having access to improved water sources.

1000+ Communities

Over 1,000 remote communities still rely on untreated water from rivers and lakes.

Climate Threat

Glacier melt in the Andes threatens future water security for millions who depend on them.

Key Challenges

Infrastructure Gap

Many rural areas lack the basic infrastructure needed to capture, treat, and distribute water effectively.

Climate Change

Rising temperatures are causing rapid glacial melt in the Andes, affecting long-term water availability.

Water Contamination

Mining activities, agricultural runoff, and industrial pollution contaminate water sources.

Economic Constraints

Many communities lack funds to build and maintain water infrastructure projects.

Geographic Barriers

Remote locations, high altitudes, and difficult terrain make infrastructure development costly and complex.

Policy Implementation

Inconsistent water management policies and enforcement across different regions.
